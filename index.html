The Problem

Rising compute costs concentrate capabilities and slow discovery. Startups pay enormous bills to cloud & GPU providers; academic labs face prohibitive costs. This reduces diversity and slows meaningful progress.

Why it matters

When compute is scarce and expensive, only the well-funded can iterate quickly. Lowering cost means more ideas, more teams, and faster breakthroughs.

Why now

Chip shortages, energy constraints, and runaway training costs make the current model unsustainable. Advances in software orchestration, scheduling, and resource sharing give us a rare opportunity to shift the economics.

Opportunity

Software-first innovations can dramatically lower marginal costs without waiting for hardware breakthroughs — democratizing research and product development.

Our Approach

We capture demand, schedule intelligently, and redistribute workloads across a secure network. The result: reduced marginal cost, reclaimed efficiency, and more available cycles for innovation.

Manifesto — excerpt

We believe intelligence should be a public good: accessible, affordable, and safe. The Kudu Labs manifesto outlines how software systems can change the economics of AI and unlock a new era of discovery.

Cost shapes research. Expensive compute narrows curiosity. We will change incentives by reducing the price of experimentation — freeing researchers to ask bolder questions.

We favor pragmatic, auditable systems: software that improves efficiency, transparency, and long-term safety. Build in public when it helps scrutiny; stay discreet where risks demand caution.
